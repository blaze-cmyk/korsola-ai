# Podcast UGC Ad

The podcast camouflage. A short-form ad styled to look like a 20–45-second clip pulled out of a real podcast episode. The viewer sees a foreground mic, a couch, mixed practical lighting, and two people (or one guest + an invisible interviewer) talking like friends — and only halfway through realizes the conversation is actually about a product.

This is the highest-trust ad format we ship. It only works if every detail feels lived-in: the mic, the lighting, the natural overlap, the disfluencies. AI-slop kills it instantly.

---

## 1. IDENTITY

- **Format:** podcast-clip UGC ad, vertical 9:16
- **Camera:** locked tripod, one continuous shot OR exactly one hard cut masked by a physical action
- **Lens feel:** ~35mm, full-body two-shot or chest-up single guest
- **Lighting:** mixed practicals (warm lamp + window edge + ambient room light) — never ring-light, never studio
- **Setting:** real interior — couch, armchair, podcast desk, dim/warm. Foreground podcast microphone is **mandatory**.
- **Cuts:** 0 or 1. If 1, mask it with an in-frame physical action (a thrown garment, a hand swipe, a lean across lens).

---

## 2. WHEN TO USE / WHEN NOT TO USE

**Use Podcast for:**
- Apparel, comfort wear, loungewear, sleepwear
- Wellness, mental-health-adjacent products
- Anything where social proof + tactile demo > visual hero
- Founder/creator products that benefit from "two friends vouching" energy

**Do NOT use Podcast for:**
- Pure visual hero products (use Hyper Motion)
- ASMR collectibles / unboxing (use Unboxing top-down)
- Cosmetics with close-up application beats (use Tutorial)
- Anything that needs macro product close-ups — the locked podcast frame can't deliver them

---

## 3. SHOT GRAMMAR

- **Two-shot composition:** both subjects fit fully inside the 9:16 frame, seated on the same couch, mic in left or right foreground, slightly out of focus.
- **Single-guest composition:** one subject seated in chair/couch facing slightly off-camera-left (toward invisible interviewer), mic in foreground, full body or chest-up.
- **Posture is proof.** Comfort-product subjects must visibly slump, sink, or nest into the seat. Posture should physically validate the spoken claim.
- **No camera moves.** No pans, no tilts, no zooms. The locked frame is what sells "real podcast."
- **No B-roll.** Stay on the master shot. (On-screen kinetic captions and brand-graphic pop-ups are allowed and encouraged.)

---

## 4. CASTING / PERSONAS

Two casting modes — pick one per script.

**Mode A — Two-person on couch (host + guest):**
- Host (asks the questions, hypes the guest): persona = `hype-friend` or `chaotic-bestie`
- Guest (delivers proof, demos product): persona = `wide-eyed-genuine`, `low-key-cool`, or `quiet-luxury`
- They overlap. They interrupt. They react physically to each other.

**Mode B — Single guest + invisible off-camera interviewer:**
- Guest on camera: any persona, but lean `low-key-cool` / `wide-eyed-genuine` for comfort products
- Interviewer is HEARD ONLY — never seen. Lines come from off-camera-left. Use the interviewer to feed lifestyle scenarios ("do you travel a lot?", "could you sleep in it?") so the guest doesn't monologue features.

---

## 5. BEAT TIMELINE TEMPLATES

### 25s two-person template (ref: Comfrt sweatpants)

```
0–2s   HOOK         Host asks one direct question. Guest answers in one word.
2–9s   VIRAL CLAIM  Host validates with a bold social-proof line, gestures.
9–11s  TACTILE      Host leans across, physically touches the product.
11–13s ACTION CUT   Guest throws a second product item across the frame (mask).
13–25s FEATURE +CTA Guest, now wearing the new piece, lists 2–3 specifics + CTA.
```

### 36s tactile-demo template (ref: Comfrt navy set)

```
0–4s   MID-SENTENCE OPEN   Guest already talking, points at one feature.
4–11s  CONTRAST PROBLEM    Guest names what other brands get wrong.
11–17s FEATURE LIST        Guest counts product variations on fingers.
17–28s TACTILE DEMO        Guest physically pulls/wears feature on camera.
28–36s SOCIAL PROOF CTA    Guest leans back, drops "all my friends are blowing me up."
```

### 44s single-guest interview template (ref: Comfrt grey/magenta sets)

```
0–7s    HOOK          Off-cam: pattern-interrupt compliment / observation. Guest answers warm.
7–15s   SETUP         Off-cam feeds a lifestyle scenario (travel, sleep, weather).
15–24s  PROOF         Guest demos with posture + low-energy hand gesture.
24–34s  STACK PROOF   Off-cam stacks a second scenario. Guest confirms with overlap.
34–44s  PIVOT + CTA   Off-cam asks about variants / colors. Guest delivers soft-intrigue close.
```

---

## 6. HOOK FORMULAS (pick exactly one)

1. **Question Hook** — Host asks a direct question that primes the product category. *"What is the most comfortable pair of sweatpants you own?"*
2. **Pattern-Interrupt Social Proof** — Off-cam interviewer drops casual recognition. *"Girl I saw you on TikTok the other day wearing the same set."*
3. **Bold Compliment Hook** — Off-cam compliments the on-screen look in a way that names the product implicitly. *"You look like an it girl right now."*
4. **Mid-Sentence Open** — Start the clip a beat after the guest has already started talking. *"And yeah, and they, this is like the first straight leg…"*
5. **Setup-then-Objection** — Guest acknowledges they almost didn't buy. *"I was gonna order it, but I didn't know…"*

---

## 7. BODY STRUCTURES (pick one)

- **Feature Cascade** — guest stacks 2–3 specifics tied to physical attributes the camera can see.
- **Objection-Reversal** — off-cam raises a buyer objection ("more pinks? brighter colors?"), guest reverses it ("girl, they have every color you could ever want").
- **Tactile-Proof Loop** — every claim is followed within 1 second by a physical action (pulling fabric, throwing the matching piece, pulling the hood up).
- **Setup → Demo → CTA** — off-cam sets up a use-case (travel, sleep), guest demonstrates with posture, drops CTA.

---

## 8. CTA PATTERNS

- **Direct** — *"You gotta get a set."*
- **Soft Intrigue** — *"Oh yeah, they have zip ups."* / *"They have every color you could ever want."*
- **Pointed Fourth-Wall** — guest locks elbow, points index finger at camera lens. One short line.
- **Social Proof Close** — *"All my friends are literally blowing me up cause I've already posted it."*

---

## 9. MANDATORY ELEMENTS (every podcast script must contain)

1. **Visible foreground podcast microphone** — described explicitly. Slightly out of focus. Black, on a boom or desktop arm.
2. **At least one tactile proof beat** — a hand pinching fabric, pulling a hood, gripping a strap, pressing a button. Within the same beat as the spoken claim it validates.
3. **Two distinct speakers** — either both on camera, or one on-camera + one heard-only off-camera. Single-monologue podcast scripts are rejected.
4. **Disfluencies** — at least three of: `like`, `cause`, `bro`, `dude`, `girl`, `wait`, `okay`, `oh`, `right?`, `I mean`. Spread naturally, not stacked.
5. **Posture/body language as proof** — for comfort/wellness products, the on-screen subject must visibly slump, sink, or nest into the seat.
6. **No mirrors. No selfies. No phone in hand.** This is a podcast clip, not a UGC selfie review.

---

## 10. ACTION-CUT TRANSITION RULE

If the script needs a wardrobe change or state change, you must mask the cut with an in-frame physical action.

- **Throw mask** — guest tosses the matching garment across the lens. Hard cut on the motion blur. New shot reveals guest now wearing it.
- **Lean mask** — guest leans deep across the frame to touch the product. Cut on the lean.
- **Hand-swipe mask** — host swipes hand past lens. Cut.

Never use a clean wipe, fade, or jump cut without a physical motivator. The action is what makes the cut invisible.

---

## 11. BANNED PATTERNS

- Green-screen look, studio backdrop, ring-light glare
- Selfie/mirror framing, phone visible in subject's hand
- "Hey guys", "today I'm reviewing", "let's take a look"
- Influencer-bright voiceover. This is conversational, not announced.
- Overhead / POV-hands / macro close-ups (those are different formats)
- Camera moves of any kind (pan, tilt, zoom, push)
- Music. Podcast clips ride on dialogue + room tone only.
- Single-monologue scripts with no second voice.
- Rigid turn-based dialogue. Real conversation overlaps.

## 14. WHAT TO STEAL FROM REAL REFERENCES

- **Podcast Camouflage** — the visible mic + couch instantly defuses ad fatigue.
- **Tactile Proof Beat** — second person physically touches the product to validate quality.
- **Action-Cut Transition** — a thrown garment masks the only hard cut.
- **Invisible Interviewer** — off-camera voice feeds lifestyle scenarios so claims emerge as discoveries, not features.
- **Posture as Proof** — sink the body to validate "feels like a blanket."
- **Mid-Sentence Open** — start a beat after talking already began; signals documentary realness.
- **Conversational Overlap** — let the two voices step on each other once. AI scripts almost never do this.
